Experience and Reflections

⁠LONG Tsz Ying Mavis

This study tour is both educational and enjoyable. Visiting renowned universities and engaging with Japanese students and researchers provided me with valuable cultural and technological insights. The highlight was Kyoto University researcher Akihiro Kubota's presentation on using generative models for strategic trajectory in table tennis. It was inspiring to see the application of computer vision and deep learning in such an innovative way!

Another memorable moment was our visit to Kanadevia A.I/TEC, where we appreciated the application of AI technology in machinery and environmental solutions. The guiding robot and real-time translating subtitles were both fun and practical solutions.

- ⁠LONG Tsz Ying Mavis

YAN Tung (Maya)

The Japan Study Tour was an enriching experience. Academically, the visits to institutions such as KU, UOsaka, KyotoU and the company Kanadevia A.I/TEC offered insightful explorations of Japan's IT industry and AI research. Meeting local students and learning about their projects was a deeply rewarding experience.

Culturally, discovering Japan's scenery while enjoying the many delicious local cuisines provided a profound cultural immersion. This journey has solidified my passion for my field of study and offered cherished memories that will undoubtedly influence my academic and personal growth.

Finally, I would like to sincerely thank the department and our professors for making this incredible journey possible.

- YAN Tung (Maya)

CHAN Wa Wai (Harry)

This trip to Japan was truly memorable. I had the chance to explore several universities and experience the vibrant campus life and the students' enthusiasm. It gave me a deeper insight into Japanese culture and society.

During my free time, I noticed many interesting differences from Hong Kong. For example, Japanese buses have passengers board from the back and exit at the front, paying when getting off—the opposite of Hong Kong. Small details like this made me appreciate how different cultures approach everyday life in unique ways. It was both eye-opening and inspiring.

- CHAN Wa Wai (Harry)

Gao Yuan (Ivy)

This study tour was an incredible experience! Visiting Kyoto University, Osaka University, and Kansai University allowed me to admire their beautiful campuses and engage in fascinating workshops on computing and robotics. The lab on computer vision and 3D image generation particularly impressed me.

Touring local tech companies provided valuable industry insights. Beyond academics, I enjoyed exploring Japanese culture—tasting delicious food, visiting Osaka Castle, and appreciating the scenic views at Shinsaibashi and Kamogawa River.

This trip was both educational and enjoyable, deepening my knowledge of computer science while creating unforgettable memories with friends. Truly a meaningful journey!

- Gao Yuan (Ivy)

Yerbolat Uzakbay (Yerbolat)

The study tour was unforgettable. At Kansai University, connecting with undergrads and sharing experiences was enlightening.Osaka University impressed me with a professor’s robotics talk on omni-directional wheels and his innovative solutions.

Kyoto University was the highlight - PhD students showcased inspiring computer vision projects, like 3D reconstruction using SPIDER and generative models for trajectory table tennis, which resonated with my graduate study aspirations. Exploring the city’s architecture, like the serene temples, savoring local cuisine such as soba and okonomyiaki, and immersing in cultural experiences, enriched the trip further.

Each university visit and city exploration deepened my appreciation for Japan’s academic and cultural landscape.

Pang Yu Hing (Tracy)

A takeaway from the tour was seeing how different school culture is there. At Kansai University, there are many buildings and facilities specialized for school clubs and societies, like an extra football penalty area, an archery court, a symphony hall, etc. This kind of dedication is rarely seen in Hong Kong.

Another big difference is that classes in Japan are often in Japanese, so they might not be as familiar with speaking English in class as we are, since many of our classes in Hong Kong are in English.

It showed me how culture impacts education and student life.

- Pang Yu Hing (Tracy)
